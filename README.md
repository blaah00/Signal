# SIGNAL
### Behavioral Entropy Model for Human Performance

**Live site:** [blaah00.github.io/signal](https://blaah00.github.io/Signal/)

---

SIGNAL detects when employees are drifting toward disengagement or attrition before it shows up in any performance metric. Most HR tools tell you what already happened. SIGNAL tells you what is about to happen — and what to do before it does.

---

## The core idea

Borrowed from the Second Law of Thermodynamics: any system naturally moves toward disorder unless energy is continuously applied. Every person in an organization experiences a gap between who they are and what the system asks them to be. That gap costs energy. Over time the energy depletes. SIGNAL measures that depletion before it becomes a resignation letter.

---

## Four Fingerprints

| Signal | Weight | What it reads |
|---|---|---|
| Thermal | 0.25 | Energy output — initiative beyond what was asked |
| Orbital | 0.35 | Collaboration gravity — the earliest pre-attrition signal |
| Resonance | 0.25 | Skill–role match — when out of phase, energy cancels |
| Transfer | 0.15 | Energy direction — thriving people give, drifting people consume |

---

## What SIGNAL outputs

- **Drift velocity** — how fast the gap is opening
- **Entropy source** — which fingerprint is failing and why
- **Minimum Effective Dose** — the smallest precise intervention to reverse the drift
- **Plain language READ** — a human summary written for a manager, not a dashboard
- **Systemic Fault Isolation** — one person drifting is individual. A whole team is the manager. Multiple teams is the system.

---

## Three Archetypes

- **Climber** — composite trending up, wiring matches the role
- **Plateauer** — score looks flat, orbital quietly declining, invisible until the resignation letter
- **ECG** — oscillating pattern, needs recognition architecture not training

---

## v3 Feature: Manager Relationship Entropy

A new score per employee measuring friction with the current manager dynamic. When aggregated across a team it powers Systemic Fault Isolation — distinguishing whether drift is coming from the individual, the manager, or the org structure.
